What does Hot Beans do?

Hot Beans is a full-service web development agency that designs, builds, and maintains high-quality websites and web applications. We specialize in custom web application development using modern technologies, helping startups and established businesses thrive online.

What technical skills do I need to work at Hot Beans?

It depends on the role, but in general we look for solid web development fundamentals. For frontend roles, we expect strong skills in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and experience with frameworks like React. For backend roles, knowledge of PHP (Laravel) or Node.js is a big plus.

How do I apply?

To apply for a position at Hot Beans. Just click the blue "Apply Here" located at the bottom of your screen. This will open a pop up where you can enter your personal and contact information, as well as upload your CV and cover letter.

What’s the hiring process like?

After we have processed your application and invited you in for an intreview. they normaly beguin with a short screening call, followed by a technical challenge or portfolio review, and then one or two interviews. We try and keep it friendly, focused, and transparent.

How long should the hiring prosses take?

If we think you are a sutible and appropiote canidate for the role. The whole prosses should take one and two weeks between the begining of the prosses, and your first day on the job.

What kind of Non-Technical skill are important at Hot Beans?

We look for people who communicate clearly, manage their time well, and aren’t afraid to ask questions or suggest improvements. If you’re detail-oriented, friendly, and genuinely care about building great web experiences, you’ll fit right in.

Is there room to grow at Hot Beans?

100%. We’re big on career development, whether you want to go deep in a technology, lead a team, or explore other areas of the business. Your growth matters here.

What kind of benefits do you offer?

Competitive pay for both senior and junior role, up to 8 weeks PTO, health coverage and dental plans, flexible working hours, equipment stipends, and company get-away's.
